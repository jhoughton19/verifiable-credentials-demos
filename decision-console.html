What this demonstration proves

  1. Recorder discretion is preserved. Even when every cryptographic check passes, the Recorder retains authority to deny a submission on policy grounds. Utah Code § 57-3-101.5(3)(c) states explicitly that nothing in the digital-authentication framework requires a county recorder to accept any digitally authenticated record.
  2. Verification is separate from decision. Cryptographic verification answers the question "is this document mathematically intact and properly signed?" The Recorder's decision answers the separate question "should this be recorded under the County's approved scope and standards?" These are not the same question.
  3. Every decision is auditable. Each approval, denial, and deferral is logged with the deciding personnel, timestamp, reason code, and a cryptographic hash of the decision itself — satisfying the audit-trail requirement of § 17-71-301.5(2)(b)(iv).
  4. The decisions themselves are tamper-evident. The hash shown to the right of each audit entry is SHA-256 of the decision payload. Decisions are immutable records, the same as the documents they govern.
